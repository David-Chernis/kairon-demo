# Instructions for Creating Kairon Pitch Deck

## Design Philosophy
This is a **startup pitch deck**, not a business report. Each slide should have ONE clear message that is readable from across a room. Think Y Combinator demo day, not a consulting presentation.

**Rules:**
- Maximum 20 words of body text per slide (headlines don't count)
- Numbers should be HUGE (60-100pt)
- No paragraphs. No bullet lists. Cards with 3-5 words max.
- Every slide passes the "3-second test": the audience gets the point in 3 seconds
- Use whitespace aggressively — empty space is a design choice
- Color palette: warm cream background (#FDFAF8), coral accent (#FF2D55), orange (#FF6B35)
- Font: Inter or similar clean sans-serif
- Cards: white with subtle warm border, rounded corners

## KEY PRINCIPLES FOR EVERY SLIDE

1. **One message per slide.** If you can't say what the slide is about in 5 words, simplify.
2. **Numbers > words.** Show "63%" not "sixty-three percent of respondents indicated..."
3. **Cards > bullets.** Three cards with 3 words each, not a bullet list with sentences.
4. **Contrast > explanation.** "Every competitor: cloud. Kairon: local." beats a paragraph about architecture.
5. **White space = confidence.** A sparse slide with one big number looks more credible than a busy slide with 10 data points.
6. **Sources are tiny.** 8pt at the very bottom. They're for credibility, not reading.
7. **No jargon.** "Data never leaves the building" not "on-premise deployment architecture with FHIR-compliant data sovereignty."

## WHAT NOT TO DO
- No paragraphs of text on any slide
- No bullet point lists with full sentences
- No 2×2 competitive quadrants (use the feature table)
- No "Made with Replit" or navigation hints
- No slide numbers (maximize content space)
- Don't explain how LLMs work
- Don't say "no competition" — say "no one does THIS locally"
- Don't inflate TAM — lead with the $1.2B SAM
